A  value  (or  principle ) usually means an  abstract   rule , one that can be seen to apply in many  experience s, or can be  apply applied  by choice in a lot of  situation s. It can also mean a  moral  choice one makes often and  consistent ly, for example, some  Buddhist s avoid eating meat as a matter of principle.

Many groups of people agree on lists of principles. They may also try to agree on the order in which they are to apply, that is, which principles should be violated before which other ones.
