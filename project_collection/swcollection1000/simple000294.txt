Maui

Maui  is the second largest of the  Hawaiian Islands , in the  United States .
It has a population of just over 100,000 and is 727 square miles (1883 km2) in size. Maui is part of  Maui County, Hawaii Maui County ,  Hawaii . The larger (or better known) towns include  Kahului ,  Wailuku ,  Lahaina ,  Hana , and  Wailea . Main industries are  agriculture  and  tourism .

Maui was named for the demi-god  Maui (mythology) Maui . In Hawaiian legend, he raised all the  Hawaiian Islands islands  from the  Pacific Ocean sea . Maui is also known as the "Valley Isle" for the large fertile isthmus (narrow land connection) between two volcanoes.

Maui is a  volcano volcanic doublet : an island formed from two volcanic mountains that are joined together. The older volcano,  West Maui Mauna Kahalawai , is much older and has been very worn down. In common talk it is called the West Maui Mountain. The larger volcano,  Haleakala , rises above 10,000 feet (3,050 m). The last eruption of Haleakala happened over 200 years ago, and this lava flow can be seen between Ahihi Bay and  La Perouse Bay  on the southeast shore.
