Periodic table

File:Periodic table.svg thumb|300px|right|The standard variation of the periodic table 
The  periodic table of the chemical elements  is a list of known  Element Elements . In the  Table (information) table , the elements are placed in the order of their  atomic number s starting with the lowest number. The atomic number of an element is the same as the number of   protons  in that particular  atom .

In the periodic table the elements are arranged into  Period (periodic table) periods  and  Group (periodic table) groups . 

A row of elements across the table is called a  period . Each period has a number: from 1 to 7. Period 1 has only 2 elements in it:  hydrogen  and  helium . Period 2 and Period 3 both have 8 elements. Other periods are longer.

A column of elements down the table is called a  group . There are 18 groups in the standard periodic table. Each group has a number: from 1 to 18. Elements in a group have electrons arranged in similar ways, which gives them similar  chemical property chemical properties  (they behave in similar ways). For example, group 18 is known as the  noble gas es because they are all gases and they do not combine with other atoms.

The periodic table can be used by  chemist s to observe patterns, and relationships between the elements. There are 3 main groups in in the Periodic Table;  metals ,  metalloids , and  gases . For example, elements to the bottom and far left of the table are the most  metal metallic , and elements on the top right are the  nonmetal least metallic . (e.g.  cesium  is much more metallic than  helium ). There are also many other patterns and relationships.

There are two systems of group numbers; one using  Hindu-Arabic numeral system Arabic numerals  (1,2,3) and the other two using  Roman numeral s (I, II, III). The Roman numeral names were used at first and are the traditional names; the Arabic numeral names are newer names that the International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry (IUPAC) decided to use as well. The IUPAC names were meant to replace the older Roman numeral systems as they used the same names to mean different things, which was confusing.

The Periodic Table was invented and arranged by the  Russia n  chemist   Dmitri Mendeleev Dmitry Ivanovich Mendeleyev  ( 1834 - 1907 ).
