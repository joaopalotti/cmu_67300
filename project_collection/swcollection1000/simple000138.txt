Et cetera

Et cetera  means "and the rest" in  Latin . It is often used in  English language English  to continue a  list  that is longer than what can be normally written. People most often write "et cetera" as  etc. . Very rarely, it is also written "&c" because the  wikt:ampersand ampersand , or the "&", is the same as "et", having been formed by  'e' and 't' being  Ligature (typography) joined  into a single letter. It is also the symbol for "and". Some people write it as "ect", but that is wrong since it incorrectly abbreviates "et cetera".

  "Robert ordered a large amount of groceries in order to stock for later. He ordered carrots, tomatoes, potatoes, eggs,  etc. "
